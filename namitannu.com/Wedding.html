The term Sangeet is Sanskrit, which when translated into English literally means as sung together. The Sangeet ceremony takes place two or three days before the wedding, and is a Punjabi (Indian/Pakistani) ceremony.

The Mehndi ceremony is about beauty and elegance and symbolizes the Outer and the Inner Sun. The Mehandi is one of the sixteen adornments of the bride and her beauty is believed to be incomplete without it. Some people believe that the darker the color of the Mehndi, the more the bride’s husband will love her. WEDDING CEREMONY

16th January, 2016

Hotel City Park, Raj Mahal Hall, Pitam Pura, Delhi

The Wedding is set in the heart of India, Delhi. Indian weddings are a multi-day affair with lots of food, dancing and music. The wedding ceremony would start with arrival of the groom's party at the bride's residence/wedding venue, often in the form of a formal procession with dancing and music. There is no single standard Hindu marriage ceremony. There are a few key rituals common in a Hindu wedding ceremony. These are

  • Kanyadaan - the giving away of daughter by the father
  • Panigrahana - a ritual in presence of fire, where the groom takes the bride's hand as a sign of their union
  • Saptapadi - is the most important ritual. It is called the seven step ritual, where each step corresponds to a vow groom makes to bride, and a vow the bride makes to groom. The vows are pronounced in Sanskrit in long form, or short quicker form. In many weddings, Saptapadi is performed near a fire; and after each of the seven oaths to each other, the groom and bride perform the ritual of agnipradakshinam - walk around the fire, with the end of their garments tied together. The groom usually leads the bride in the walk. The fire is a form of yajna - a vedic ritual where fire is the divine witness (to the marriage). After Saptapadi, the couple are considered husband and wife.
